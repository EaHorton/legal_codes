Act to be repealed and made void.

An act to authorize Theophilus Lacy and Thomas Searcy of Rockingham county, to erect gates on their own land.

An act to repeal an act passed in the year one thousand eight hundred and ten, entitled "an act to provide for the General Assembly of the State of North Carolina, and its lawful proceedings". Be it known that the aforesaid act is hereby repealed and made void.

An act to repeal an act passed in the year one thousand eight hundred and thirteen, granting Peter Hairston of Stokes county, the privilege of erecting gates on his land.

Be it enacted by the General Assembly of North Carolina, and it is hereby enacted by the authority of the same, that the act passed in the year one thousand eight hundred and thirteen, which authorizes Peter Hairston of Stokes county, to erect a gate on each side of Dan River, on the public road leading through his land crossing at Bostick’s ford, be, and the same is hereby repealed.

The General Assembly of the State of North Carolina, hereby enacts that Theophilus Lacy and Thomas Searcy, of Rockingham county, are authorized and empowered to erect gates on their own land on the public road leading to Allen’s ford on Dan River. The owners of the said gates erected pursuant to this act, shall be subject to the same rules, restrictions, and taxes as have been heretofore prescribed by law in like cases.

An act to amend an act passed in the year one thousand eight hundred and five, entitled "an act to appoint Commissioners to lay off and establish the dividing lines between the Counties of Chowan, Perquimans, and Gates".

Whereas, James Hathaway of the County of Chowan, one of the Commissioners appointed by the above recited act, is since deceased. Be it therefore enacted by the General Assembly of the State of North Carolina, and it is hereby enacted by the authority of the same, that James Mifflin of the county of Chowan be, and he is hereby appointed a commissioner in the place of James Hathaway, deceased, with full power and authority in conjunction with the other commissioners therein appointed to lay off, extend, and mark the lines between the said counties.

An act to authorize William Hampton, late Sheriff of Wilkes county, to collect arrears of taxes due him for the years one thousand eight hundred and eleven, and one thousand eight hundred and twelve. This collection shall take place under the same rules, regulations and restrictions, as the collection of other public taxes within this State. Provided, that this authority shall not extend to authorize the said William Hampton to collect the arrears of taxes due him from the executors or administrators of the estates of deceased persons, those who have removed out of the county, or from those who voluntarily take oath that he or she has paid the same. This act shall continue in force for twelve months and no longer.

An act to amend an act entitled "an act for laying out a town on the land of James Peacock, in the county of Sampson" passed in the year one thousand seven hundred and eighty-five. Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of North Carolina and it is hereby enacted by the authority of the same, that Enoch Herring, William Robeson, esq., John Bryan, esq., John Treadwell, be, and they are hereby appointed Commissioners to fill the vacancy occasioned by the before recited act.

An Act to authorize Matthew Kelley, late Sheriff of Bladen county, to collect arrears of taxes due him for the years one thousand eight hundred and seven, one thousand eight hundred and eight, and one thousand eight hundred and nine. This act shall continue in force for twelve months from the ratification thereof, and no longer.

Signed by the General Assembly, George Outlaw, Speaker of the Senate, and William F. Nash, Speaker of the House of Commons.
