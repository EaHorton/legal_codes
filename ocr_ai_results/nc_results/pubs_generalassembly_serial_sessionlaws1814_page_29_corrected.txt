CHAP, LXXXI, 4

An act to prevent any person or persons from impeding the free passage of Fish up Brices Creek in the County of Craven and other purposes:

Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of North Carolina, and it is hereby enacted by the

authority of the same, That from and after the passing of this act, it shall not be lawful for any person
or persons to set or cause to be set any net of any description across Brices Creek in the county of

Craven, under the penalty of twenty for each offence, to be recovered by any person suing for
the same, to his or her own use, before any jurisdiction having cognizance thereof,

II. Be it further enacted, That if any servant or slave, shall be guilty of the aforementioned offence, without the knowledge or consent of his or her master or mistress, he or she so offending shall have and
receive thirty-nine lashes on his or her bare back, etc. ;

II. Be it further enacted, That the wardens of the poor of Craven county, may annually lay a tax
on the inhabitants of the said county, not exceeding one shilling and six pence on every taxable poll,
and six pence on every hundred acres of land; and one shilling and six pence on every hundred pounds
value of town property, in addition to the taxes which the said wardens have been heretofore empowered to lay, for the support of the poor ; and the taxes to be raised in pursuance of this act, shall be
collected, accounted for, and applied in the manner heretofore prescribed by law.

CHAP. LXXXII.

An act to amend an act entitled “an act to incorporate a company to be called the Clubfoot and Harlow's Creek Company.

Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of North Carolina, and it

is hereby enacted by the authority of the same, That the Commissioners appointed by said act be and

are hereby authorized to keep open the books for the purpose of receiving subscriptions to the stock of the said Company, until a sufficient amount of stock is subscribed.

II. Be it further enacted, That so much of the second section of the said act as provides, that unless three-fourths of the stock is subscribed, all subscriptions made in pursuance of the act shall be void,
and the money paid on such subscriptions shall be refunded to the respective subscribers, be, and the same is hereby repealed and made void.

III. Be it further enacted, That this act shall be in force immediately from and after the passing thereof.

CHAP. LXXXIII.

An act to amend an act passed at the last session of the General Assembly, entitled an act to secure to the persons therein mentioned such property as they may hereafter acquire.

Whereas it is represented to this General Assembly, that through a mistake committed in the
enactment of the said act, such property as might be thereafter acquired by Alee Beck of Burke county, was
secured to her, free from the claims of her husband, which name should have been Alee Banks, wife of
William Banks, by which mistake the petitioner was deprived of the benefit intended by said act.

Be it therefore enacted by the General Assembly of the State of North Carolina, and it is hereby enacted by the authority of the same, That all property, either real or personal, which may be hereafter
acquired by Alee Banks of Burke county, wife of William Banks, be and the same is hereby secured
to her, free from the claim or claims of her said husband, in as full and ample a manner
as if the rites of matrimony had never been solemnized between them. And the said property so acquired by the said Alee Banks as aforesaid, shall be held, used, and enjoyed by her in her own proper name.

IV. Be it further enacted, That the said Alee Banks is hereby declared to be able and capable in law
to contract and be contracted with, to sue and be sued, to plead and be impleaded, in any court of law or equity, by her own proper name as aforesaid, to acquire, hold and dispose of property, both real and personal, and to exercise all such other rights and powers, as any femme sole might or could do, as fully and effectually as if she were unmarried, and as if this act had never been passed—any law, usage or custom, to the contrary notwithstanding.
