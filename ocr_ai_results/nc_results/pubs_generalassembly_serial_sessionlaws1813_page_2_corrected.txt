Chapter I

An Act authorizing the County Court of Wilkes to Tax for the purpose of building a Gaol

Be it enacted by the General Assembly of North Carolina, and it is hereby enacted by the authority of the same, That it shall be lawful for the Court of Wilkes to levy a tax for the years one thousand eight hundred and fourteen, one thousand eight hundred and fifteen, and one thousand eight hundred and sixteen, should a majority of the court deem it proper and necessary, not exceeding two shillings on every poll, not exceeding two shillings on every hundred acres of land, and not exceeding two shillings on every hundred pounds' worth of town Lots and their improvements. And when said tax is laid, it shall be levied, collected and accounted for in like manner as other taxes.

And be it further enacted, That when the Sheriff of said county shall collect the taxes, he shall pay the monies so collected over to the undertaker of said building, and his receipt shall be a sufficient voucher in his settlement with the County Trustee.

And be it further enacted, That the Commissioners shall let out the building to the lowest bidder, advertising at least one month previous to letting out the building, specifying therein particularly the length, breadth, how to be built, and of what kind.

And be it further enacted, That as soon as the company Acre Hacket, Jesse Robinett, Esquire, and Francis are appointed commissioners for a project within the bounds of the town of Wilkes, they shall require security from the undertaker or builders of said public building, when laid down as aforesaid.

And be it further enacted, That the said commissioners as herein appointed shall, on the twentieth day of June next, proceed to let out the building of the Gaol to the lowest bidder, and the undertaker or undertakers thereof shall execute the work agreeably to the plan agreed upon and within the time set forth in the publication of their notice.

Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of North Carolina, that from and after the passing of this act, the bonds of matrimony between Anna and Seth are hereby dissolved, and they are hereby declared to be as single and unmarried for all intents and purposes, as if the marriage between the said Anna and Seth had never been solemnized. Provided that the issue, if any, of the said marriage shall be legitimate.

Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of North Carolina, that the former survey of said land in the town of Henderson, or the dedication thereof, is hereby confirmed.

Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of North Carolina, that the said commissioners, after having made such survey, shall report the same, with their proceedings thereon, to the next Court.
