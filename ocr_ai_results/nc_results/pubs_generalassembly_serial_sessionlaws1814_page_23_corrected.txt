CHAP. L.

An act to provide for a settlement with the Court Officers of Buncombe county.

Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of North Carolina, and hereby enacted by the authority of the same, That George Swain, James Wittker, be, and they are hereby appointed Commissioners, with full power to make a settlement with all delinquent Sheriffs, county Trustees, Wardens of the poor, and all other officers entrusted with public money for the county of Buncombe, from the first day of July one thousand eight hundred and four, to the first day of January one thousand eight hundred and fifteen; and after that period annually, in the presence of the county court.

And be it further enacted, That the said Commissioners may hold their meeting at the Court House, and shall at their first meeting or any subsequent, issue summonses signed by them, to all delinquents, who on being summoned, in case of death of the officer, his legal representatives, to render the Commissioners at the time and place specified, a true account of the public monies by them collected or received, during the aforesaid periods and annually thereafter.

And be it further enacted, That it shall be the duty of the Commissioners to procure all testimony, either oral or written, which comes within their cognizance, and for this purpose shall have the same power to compel the attendance of witnesses by subpoena, as is now exercised by courts of record within this State.

And be it further enacted, That all subpoenas by them issued, shall be directed to the coroner, whose duty it shall be to execute the same at least five days previous to the meeting of said Commissioners.

And be it further enacted, That at the first county court to be held after the said Commissioners shall have completed their investigation and annually thereafter, it shall be their duty to exhibit a list of all balances due from any officer or officers by the said Commissioners respectively, and it shall be the duty of the said court immediately after receiving the same, to require their clerk to issue, in the name of the chairman, a scire facias or scire faciases to such delinquent officer or officers and their securities, directed to the sheriff of the county wherein such delinquent shall reside; but in case such scire facias shall be issued against the sheriff for the time being, then it shall be the duty of the clerk to issue the same to the coroner, requiring such delinquent or delinquents to appear at the next ensuing term, to show cause why judgment shall not be rendered against him or them for the amount reported by said Commissioners, and if on the service and return of such process, the defendant or defendants, shall fail to appear and make defense, final judgment by default, shall be entered against him or them, and in every case, the report of the Commissioners shall be prima facia evidence upon which said court shall give judgment, unless it shall be rebutted by evidence on the part of the defendant or defendants.

And be it further enacted, That it shall be the duty of the attorney, acting in behalf of the State for the said county, to prosecute all suits instituted under this act, who shall be allowed the same fees as in other cases of scire facias, and no process issued under this act shall be abated or set aside for any informality.

And be it further enacted, That the clerk of the county court of said county shall keep a book.
