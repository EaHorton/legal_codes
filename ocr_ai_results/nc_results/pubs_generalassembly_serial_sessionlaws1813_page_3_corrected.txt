Laws of North Carolina.

CHAPTER LXKXL.
An Act to appoint Commissioners for the better regulation of the Town of Lamberton.
BE it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of North Carolina, and it is hereby enacted by the authority of the same, That William Ash Taylor, Zachariah Jordan, Charles Remon, M'Neil and Richard C., be and they are hereby appointed Commissioners of the Town of Lumberton, and they are hereby vested with full power and authority to adopt such measures as they, or a majority of them, may deem expedient for the good regulation and governance of the town. 

1. Be it further enacted, That the death, resignation, inability or removal of any of the commissioners aforesaid, it shall be lawful for the remaining commissioners, or a majority of them, to appoint a successor who shall have equal authority and power with the commissioners hereby appointed.

CHAPTER LXXXIII.
An Act for the relief of Maurice Jones, late Sheriff of the county of Hyde.
BE it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of North Carolina, and it is hereby enacted by the authority of the same, That Maurice Jones, late sheriff of the county of Hyde, be and he is hereby authorised and empowered for and during the term of one year from and after the ratification of this act, to collect all arrearages of taxes due to him for the year one thousand eight hundred and eleven, from persons owning taxable property in said county; and for the purpose of effecting such collection, he is vested with all the powers and privileges given by law to sheriffs for the purpose of collecting taxes. Provided always, That he shall not be authorised by virtue of this act to collect any arrearages of taxes from any person or persons who will make oath that he, she or they have paid the same.

CHAPTER LXXXIII.
An Act to amend an act passed in one thousand eight hundred and eleven, entitled "An act to appoint Commissioners to contract with Thomas B. Littlejohn for fifty acres of land to erect a Town upon, and for other purposes."
BE it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of North Carolina, and it is hereby enacted by the authority of the same, That the time limited in the sixth section of the before recited act for improving certain lots therein named, be extended to two years from the expiration of the term specified in said act, anything to the contrary notwithstanding.

CHAPTER GXKXIV.
An Act to divorce Stephen Gilmour and his wife Charity.
BE it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of North Carolina, and it is hereby enacted by the authority of the same, That the marriage which exists between Stephen and Charity is hereby absolutely and entirely dissolved to all intents and purposes.

CHAPTER EXKXV.
An Act to remove the Election from Morrison's Meeting-house, in Burke county.
BE it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of North Carolina, and it is hereby enacted by the authority of the same, That John Higgins and Benjamin Brackett are hereby appointed Managers for the purposes herein after mentioned. 

1. And be it further enacted, That it shall be the duty of the said Managers, or a majority of them, on or before the first day of June next, to fix upon a suitable place for holding a separate Election in said county of Burke; and after fixing upon the same, shall communicate their determination to the Court of Pleas and Quarter Sessions of said county at July sessions, one thousand eight hundred and fourteen; and at the place so fixed upon, a separate election for the purpose of electing Members of the General Assembly, Representatives in Congress, and to vote for President and Vice-President of the United States, shall be held at the times fixed by law and under the same rules and regulations as other elections are held in the said county. 

2. Be it further enacted, That the election heretofore held at Morrison's Meeting-house, as aforesaid, be hereafter discontinued.

CHAPTER LXXKVL.
An Act for the regulation of the Wilmington Academy.
BE it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of North Carolina, and it is hereby enacted by the authority of the same, That the Trustees of the Wilmington Academy, or a majority of them, at any meetings subsequent to the passing of this law, shall have full power to elect by ballot such additional trustees as they may think proper, and to make such rules and regulations as to them may seem necessary for the good government of the trustees of said Academy.

1. Be it further enacted, That in case of death, resignation or refusal to act, the trustees at a regular meeting, seven or more being present, may elect others to supply the places of those who may die, resign or refuse to act.

CHAPTER LXXXVII.
An Act to authorise the County Court of Pasquotank to appoint a Public Measurer of Grain and Salt for the Town of Elizabeth.
BE it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of North Carolina, and it is hereby enacted by the authority of the same, That the County Court of Pasquotank shall and they are hereby authorised to appoint a public measurer of grain and salt for the Town of Elizabeth. 

1. Provided, That the person so appointed shall, previous to his entering on the duties of his appointment, give bond with security, which shall be approved by the court aforesaid, for the faithful and impartial discharge of his duties, and that his measures shall correspond with the standard which is prescribed by the present existing laws; and he shall receive such compensation for his services as the court aforesaid may direct, one half to be paid by the deliverer and the other half by the receiver of Grain and Salt. Provided however, That this act shall not affect farmers who may deliver grain to merchants and others within the said city.

CHAPTER LXXXVIII.
An Act to authorise the County Court of Macon to lay and collect a tax for the benefit of the Poor of said county.
BE it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of North Carolina, and it is hereby enacted by the authority of the same, That from and after the passing of this act, it shall and may be lawful for the County Court of Macon, at any time they may think proper, to lay a tax not exceeding two shillings on every hundred acres of land, and two shillings on every hundred pounds worth of town property, to defray the expenses of the poor of said county."
