III. Be it enacted, that Kirkendale is authorized to appoint five individuals who shall oversee the said road in accordance with the regulations and laws provided by the state.

George B. Greer, along with the aforementioned individuals, shall be responsible for the maintenance and regulation of the said road. 

It is hereby enacted that the proprietors of the aforementioned properties shall be allowed a period of five years to perform the tasks as prescribed in the above-mentioned act. 

The proprietors are required to keep the road in repair and in a satisfactory state for the convenience of the public.

From and after the passing of this act, the proprietors are hereby authorized to collect a toll not exceeding the standard rate for every black poll between the designated points on the road.

The proprietors are also required to keep a detailed account of the toll receipts and expenditures. This account shall be made public at the county court house under the supervision of said commissioners. 

Half of the toll collected shall be used for the maintenance of the road and the rest shall be for the benefit of the person suing for the same. Provided that a detailed statement should have been made public as per the law.

VI. And be it further enacted, that no person shall be eligible as a proprietor if not seized, or possessed, or engaged in the same.

CHAP. XXII.

An act to establish one other separate Election in the County of Orange.

BE it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of North Carolina, and it is hereby enacted by the authority of the same, that in future, an election shall be held at the house of Andrew in Orange county, on the second Thursday in August in each and every year, for the purpose of electing members of the General Assembly, representatives to Congress, and Electors to vote for President and Vice President of the United States, whenever the same shall be required by law. This election shall be under the same rules, acts and restrictions as other separate elections are held in said county.

CHAP. XXXII.

An Act for the removal of a Separate Election in the county of Rockingham.

Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of North Carolina, and it is hereby enacted by the authority of the same, that in future, the separate election which has been heretofore established in the county of Rockingham shall be removed.
