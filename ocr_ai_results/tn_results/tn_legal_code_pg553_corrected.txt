$1. An Act to authorize the clerks of the county and circuit courts, 1819, 
"to build an office on part of the public square in the town of Blountville."
(Passed October 7, 1819.)

Enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Tennessee, 
the clerks of the county and circuit courts of Sullivan are hereby authorized to build an office 
on the public square, in the town of Blountville, as designated by the county court of said county. 
Upon the death or resignation of said clerks, or either of them, the subsequent clerks, or either of them, 
may succeed to the vacancy by reason of said death or resignation. They shall have the benefit of said offices or either 
of them, as the case may be, upon paying the clerk or clerks, or their heirs, in case of death, the value of said offices, 
any law to the contrary notwithstanding. 
This act shall be in force from and after its passage.

An Act to appropriate the amount of a fine imposed on Benjamin 
by the county court of Davidson county.

An Act to legalize the judicial acts of Cullen Carlee. (Passed October 21, 1819.)

Cullen Carlee was appointed a justice of the peace for Rutherford county, by the General Assembly in 1817, 
but by mistake, he was commissioned by the name of Cullen Carley; therefore,
Enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Tennessee, 
all judicial acts of the said Cullen Carlee, who was mistakenly appointed and commissioned by the name of Cullen Carley, 
are hereby ratified and confirmed. 
That said Cullen Carlee is hereby continued in his appointment of justice of the peace for Rutherford county, 
and shall have full power and authority to do all acts, and enjoy all the privileges and emoluments he would 
have been entitled to had he been appointed and commissioned correctly.
